Baby Penguins

Penguin babies, called "chicks," are covered in fuzzy down feathers which help to keep them warm. They look quite furry - but this isn't fur - it is feathers.

  • Tallest and Heaviest: Emperor Penguin can grow up to 4'4" tall and weigh as much as 51lbs.

  • Diet: Baby chicks eat fish, krill, and squid that are regurgitated from the mother.

  • Kidnappings: Some mother penguins will try to kidnap baby penguin chicks because their chick was lost or died.

  • Starvation:Father Emperor Penguins immediately head back out to the open ocean to feed for the next 24 days. Upon return he will feed the baby chick and the mother will go without feeding.

Penguins (order Sphenisciformes , family Spheniscidae) are a group of aquatic flightless birds. Highly adapted for life in the water, penguins have countershaded dark and white plumage and flippers for swimming. Most penguins feed on krill, fish, squid and other forms of sea life which they catch while swimming underwater. They spend roughly half of their lives on land and the other half in the sea.
